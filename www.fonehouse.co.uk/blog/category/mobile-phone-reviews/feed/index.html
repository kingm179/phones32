]]> The Sony Xperia 10 IV 5G has been labelled as the “perfect companion for everyday life”. You might be wondering what exactly does that mean? Well, in this new handsets case, this means a long-lasting 5,000mAh battery great for long days of adventure, a 6.0” illuminating OLED display destined for streaming perfection, High-Resolution Audio for a concert-like listening experience and so much more. I put these claims to the test by taking this shiny new handset with me on a London day out (a very long one at that). Will this lightweight Sony handset live up to the hype? Keep scrolling to find out. 

Feature 1: 6.0” 21:9 Wide OLED Display 

We all want a display that injects life into everything we do on our devices, whether that’s endless social media scrolling, streaming your favourite guilty pleasure TV show or even engaging in some quality gaming time. The 6.0” Sony Xperia 10 IV 5G display has a lot of cool features under its belt. To name a few a 21:9 FHD+ OLED TRILUMINOS Display for breathtaking colour and contrast and a 2520 x 1080 resolution for crystal clear visuals. 

Feature 1: Display Test

If you’re a film fanatic you’re probably used to heading to the likes of Youtube to check out the latest trailers that are fresh on the scene and to test the Sony Xperia 10 IV 5G display I did just that. This time I watched the Woman Is King Trailer- a highly anticipated film due to be released in October 2022.

Feature 1: Result 

As soon as I hit play I was blown away by the magic the TRILUMINOS® display the Sony Xperia 10 IV 5G had to offer. The truest golds, deepest greens and boldest purples were showcased and the large 6”0 screen size only added to the experience further. There is a reason why Sony are known for their expertise in remarkable displays. The handset also fitted comfortably in hand due to its compact design and super lightweight feel. 

Feature 2: Triple Lens Rear Camera 

An exceptional camera is a requirement for the majority of modern-day smartphone users (I mean how else will we snap all of our wonderful Instagram content?). This Sony device’s triple rear camera has been described as “feature-packed” and “breathtaking”. This camera set-up consists of a 16mm 8MP ultra-wide-angle lens, a 27mm 12MP wide-angle lens and lastly a 54mm 8MP telephoto lens. 

Feature 2: Camera Test

Since I spent the weekend exploring all the magic London has to offer from views to food, I thought this was the perfect opportunity to use the Sony Xperia 10 IV 5G to capture it in all its glory. 

Feature 2: Result

Brick Lane in East London was my first stop, where I was hit with a beautiful range of colourful street art and graffiti which I just had to snap some photos of. The Sony Xperia 10 IV 5G effortlessly picked up all the vibrancy and light within the shot thanks to the trio of lenses and incredible LED flash. If posting art across your social media platforms is your thing, this is the perfect camera for you. 

My second stop with the Sony Xperia by my side was the South African restaurant Shaka Zulu in Camden Market. Thanks to Optical Image Stabilisation, Night Mode and Auto HDR, my photos were blur-free, stable and clear despite the restaurant’s very low lighting conditions. 

All the beautiful colours of the dishes were picked up at exceptional quality, and again the incredible LED flash illuminated the shot efficiently. A photo so good you can almost taste it.

Feature 3: Powerful 5,000mAh Battery For All Day Power 

A long-lasting battery is essential for any long day out, I mean there’s music to be played, photos to be taken and videos to be streamed. Thankfully the Sony Xperia 10 IV 5G is equipt with a large capacity 5,000mAh battery giving users much-needed ‘all-day power’. 

Feature 3: Battery Test

Leaving my house at 11 AM with the Sony handset by my side, and not getting back home until 11 PM that night, gave this handset a solid 12 hours test to see if the claim of ‘all-day power’ had some proof behind it. 

Feature 3: Result

Starting on a 100% charge and after 12 hours of photo-taking, bursts of gaming and music-playing, the Xperia device’s 5,000mAh battery only dropped by 12% – not bad at all for a full day’s work. Life with this smartphone means using a power bank won’t be a regular occurrence at all. Looks like the ‘All Day Power’ statement has proof in the pudding after all.

Feature 4: Premium Sound 

Sony and premium sound come hand in hand. For a truly authentic listening experience, the 10 IV 5G is equipt with High-Resolution Audio (which gives you premium sound), 360 Reality Audio Upmix (which works with local audio files and streaming services to further enhance your listening experience) and a 3.5mm audio jack (perfect for on the go streaming). 

Feature 4: Sound Test

As a music lover, I was excited to test out the audio quality on this device. The golden question was would the experience be mini-concert worthy and premium? To answer this burning question I headed to Spotify, pressed a feel-good song and listened away. 

Feature 4: Result

The audio quality from this handset was one of a kind. The 360 Reality Upmix feature in particular created an immersive experience in real-time. Achieving premium quality sound on this device is done with ease. 

Ultimately I found the 10 IV 5G very impressive. Not only were the acclaimed stand-out features regarding the display, camera, sound and battery true, but they actually exceeded my expectations.

Hidey Hole

Loving the new infinity display, but thinking that the punch holes are ruining it a little, then this is the app for you. This app provides you with a curated list of wallpapers that are designed with the punch holes in mind, and are designed to hide them easily within the design. This means that despite them still being there, you no longer notice them.

This app is constantly updated so you will always find something good to choose from.

Holey Light

One of the things that having an infinity display does is eliminates the notification LED. Which some people are missing as a quick visual indicator as to what is going on. If you are one of those people then this is for you. What the app does is change the colour of the edge of the punch hole camera to pulse with different colours depending on the notifications. This effectively gives you a notification led, but makes use of the punch hole camera to do so.

Battery Ring

This is yet another app that does something different with the punch hole in the display for the camera. This time, it lights up partially around the edge as a visual indicator of how much battery life is left on your phone. With tones of customisation options for colours etc, this a great visual indicator of when you need to charge your phone, or when it is done charging on your bedside table.

Borderlight

This one isn’t about the punch hole thankfully, but instead what this does is give your entire phone a colourful changing border around your screens edge. Think of it as a more colourful replacement to the edge lighting feature that is found on the phone. This gives a great effect and is in keeping with the current trend in accessories on gaming devices, so maybe this just one for the games in the audience.

Sound Assistant

This is unique in that it is an app directly from Samsung, and once installed on your phone it allows you more granular control over how your sound works. It allows you to adjust the sound volume for apps individually as well as change what the volume buttons control, be it media, or call volumes. This is an app that should have been there by default, but as such it is a nice addition.

AdHell3

AdHell3 is one of those useful apps that blocks ads system wide on the phone, it runs as a service in the background, doesn’t require root, and it doesn’t drain your battery, which is fantastic and something that really gives you great control over your phone and saves your data.

]]> The Galaxy S9’s camera so a leap ahead in terms of the technology. It features a number of innovations, that mean that your photos really pop, and shine in the right light.

The Basics

On the S9 you get a single lens, dual aperture rear camera. It is a 12-megapixel wide angle lens and has f/1.5 and f/2.4 variable apertures. This also has an auto-focus lens. It is also capable of capturing video in full 4K UHD at 60 fps, and you also get the super slow-motion video capture mode, this will take video at 960 fps. On the front there is an 8-megapixel camera that is great for taking selfies, and also makes your video calls look really great.

Performance

The dual aperture aspect of the lens is designed to improve performance of the camera in low light levels. The selective focus mode uses the dual aperture to bring attention to the object of your picture, whilst blurring out the background. This is a really clever trick, and the dual aperture means that in low light levels you can still get really good results and take great pictures without using the flash.

Low Light

Low light has been the focus by Samsung when it came to the camera this time. They wanted to make sure that when you take a photo, that it doesn’t appear washed out or over exposed. Whilst the camera uses automatic settings by default. You can change these manually to ensure that you get the best photo. This includes selecting which aperture to use. You can also switch the phone into Pro mode, and this gives you manual settings for every option. This requires some experimentation, but you can get some great results. The use of HDR also makes sure that the darkest areas of your phone really pops.

Photo Software

There are a number of great features included in the camera software, that lets you do many different things. There is an option called AR Emoji, and what this does is use your image to create a number of cartoon animated gifs that you can send to your friends instead of regular emojis. The camera software itself it set up to run everything automatically by default, and this means that most of the time you get the best possible pictures. It includes choosing which aperture to use, and it seamlessly switches between the two depending on the shot. If you find this a little over zealous, then you can use the Pro Mode to override it.

Conclusion

The camera has been greatly improved on the Galaxy S8 and it is so much better than the one on the Galaxy S8. You can spend hours and hours playing with all the different options and changing the settings, whilst you get a load of really good photos. So, whether you are trying to get into photography, or just want to up your selfie game and become the envy of social media. The options are there for you.
